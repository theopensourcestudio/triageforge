# Governance

## Roles

- **Primary maintainer:** owns releases, security coordination, project direction, and final merge decisions.
- **Core maintainer:** may triage issues, review pull requests, and merge within agreed areas.
- **Contributor:** proposes changes through issues and pull requests.

## Decision making

Routine changes use lazy consensus after tests and review. Security-sensitive, privacy-sensitive, release, governance, and automatic-write features require explicit primary-maintainer approval. Disagreement should be recorded in the relevant issue with alternatives and evidence.

## Becoming a maintainer

Sustained high-quality contributions, respectful review, reliability, and understanding of the safety boundaries are required. Maintainer access is never granted solely for volume of commits.

## Inactivity and succession

A maintainer may step down at any time. Access should be removed when no longer needed. If the primary maintainer becomes unavailable, active core maintainers may appoint a successor by documented consensus.
